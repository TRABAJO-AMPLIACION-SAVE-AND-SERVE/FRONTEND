How Save & Serve Works

Step 1: We Collect the Food

Save & Serve collects unused food from restaurants and supermarkets that would otherwise be discarded, helping reduce food waste.

See more details
Step 2: Food Logistics and Preparation

Once the food is collected, Save & Serve takes care of sorting, storing, and properly preparing it for distribution. We ensure that all food is handled safely and maintains its quality to be delivered to those in need.

See more details
Step 3: Direct Delivery to Those in Need

The prepared and stored food is delivered directly to people and communities facing food insecurity. Through our distribution network, we work to ensure every meal reaches those who need it most.

Get your questions answered

Save and Serve aims to reduce food waste by collecting surplus food from supermarkets and restaurants and redistributing it to charities and people in need, contributing to the global fight against hunger.

We partner with businesses to collect unsold but edible food. Our logistics team ensures the food is transported safely and efficiently to local charities, food banks, and individuals.

The donations go to charities, shelters, food banks, and directly to families and individuals in need. We work closely with community organizations to ensure the food reaches the right people.

You can join our community by signing up for our newsletter, participating in local events, or volunteering for our initiatives. Visit the "Get Involved" section for more details.

We provide detailed reports to our partners, showing how much food was donated, where it went, and the number of people impacted. Our goal is to build trust and accountability.

We collect fresh produce, packaged goods, and prepared meals that meet food safety standards but are no longer suitable for sale. Perishable and non-perishable items are both accepted.

If food is not suitable for donation, we explore sustainable disposal methods like composting to minimize waste and its environmental impact.

Absolutely. We adhere to strict food safety guidelines to ensure that all donations are handled, stored, and transported properly to maintain quality and safety.
